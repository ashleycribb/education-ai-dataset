# AITA Platform Integration with North Carolina Department of Public Instruction Standards

## Executive Summary

This analysis examines how the AITA (AI Teaching Assistant) platform can be integrated with North Carolina Department of Public Instruction (NCDPI) standards, policies, and educational frameworks. The analysis covers current NC educational standards, technology integration requirements, assessment protocols, and specific integration opportunities for the AITA system.

## üìã Current North Carolina Educational Framework

### 1. North Carolina Standard Course of Study (NCSCOS)

**Overview**: The NCSCOS defines content standards for each grade level and high school course, providing uniform learning standards for all NC public schools.

**Key Components**:
- **Subject Areas**: English Language Arts, Mathematics, Science, Social Studies, Arts Education, CTE, Computer Science, World Languages
- **Grade Levels**: K-12 with specific standards for each grade
- **Review Cycle**: Standards reviewed every 5-7 years
- **Current Status**: ELA standards under revision (2025), Math standards entering revision phase

**AITA Integration Opportunities**:
- Align AI tutoring content with specific NCSCOS standards
- Generate practice questions based on grade-level competencies
- Track student progress against state learning objectives
- Provide standards-aligned remediation and enrichment

### 2. Literacy Instruction Standards (LIS)

**Framework**: Defines quality and equity standards for literacy instruction across all grade bands.

**Grade Bands**:
- Pre-K
- Grades K-2
- Grades 3-5
- Grades 6-8
- Grades 9-12

**Key Focus Areas**:
- **Foundational Skills**: Phonics, fluency, vocabulary
- **Comprehension**: Discussion, writing, strategy instruction, knowledge-building
- **Writing**: Explicit instruction and meaningful opportunities
- **Assessment**: Ongoing observation and responsive instruction
- **Engagement**: Authentic and relevant literacy experiences

**AITA Integration**:
- Provide personalized literacy instruction aligned with LIS framework
- Support vocabulary development with context-aware explanations
- Generate writing prompts and provide feedback aligned with grade-band expectations
- Offer comprehension strategy instruction through interactive dialogue

### 3. Digital Learning Standards

**Foundation**: Based on International Society for Technology in Education (ISTE) Standards for Students

**Implementation**: Began 2020-2021 school year, delivered by classroom teachers across all curricular areas

**ISTE Student Standards**:
1. **Empowered Learner**: Students leverage technology to take an active role in choosing, achieving, and demonstrating competency in their learning goals
2. **Digital Citizen**: Students recognize the rights, responsibilities, and opportunities of living, learning, and working in an interconnected digital world
3. **Knowledge Constructor**: Students critically curate a variety of resources using digital tools
4. **Innovative Designer**: Students use a variety of technologies within a design process
5. **Computational Thinker**: Students develop and employ strategies for understanding and solving problems
6. **Creative Communicator**: Students communicate clearly and express themselves creatively
7. **Global Collaborator**: Students use digital tools to broaden their perspectives and enrich their learning

**AITA Alignment**:
- Support all seven ISTE standards through AI-powered learning experiences
- Promote digital citizenship through safe, ethical AI interactions
- Enhance computational thinking through problem-solving dialogues
- Foster creative communication through AI-assisted writing and projects

## üîß Technology Integration Requirements

### 1. Third-Party Data Integration Standards

**NCDPI Requirements** (Effective January 1, 2024):
- **Data Security Compliance**: Must align with NC Department of Information Technology (DIT) standards
- **Required Documentation**:
  - Data Confidentiality and Security Agreement
  - Third Party Data Collection Reporting Worksheet
  - Security assessment documentation
- **Canvas LTI Integration Deadline**: Extended to August 1, 2024
- **Recommended LTI Version**: LTI v1.3 (v1.1 will be deprecated)

**AITA Compliance Strategy**:
- Implement LTI v1.3 integration for Canvas compatibility
- Complete NCDPI third-party integration process
- Ensure NIST 800-53 framework compliance
- Provide required security documentation

### 2. Student Data Privacy and Security

**Legal Framework**:
- Article 29 of NC General Statute 115C (student data protection)
- FERPA compliance requirements
- State of North Carolina Information Security Manual alignment

**AITA Implementation**:
- Encrypt all student data in transit and at rest
- Implement role-based access controls
- Provide audit trails for all data access
- Ensure data minimization and purpose limitation
- Offer data portability and deletion capabilities

### 3. Learning Management System Integration

**Primary Systems in NC**:
- **Canvas**: Widely adopted across NC districts
- **PowerSchool**: Student Information System
- **Home Base**: NCDPI's student data platform

**AITA Integration Points**:
- Single Sign-On (SSO) through district authentication systems
- Grade passback via LTI for assignments and assessments
- Roster synchronization with SIS systems
- Progress reporting integration with existing dashboards
